> - **Challenge** 17 (18,000 XP)
> - **Proficiency Bonus** +6
>
> ___
> ***Amphibious.*** The dragon turtle can breathe air and water.
> 
> ***Elemental.*** Each lion turtle is connected to one of the four elements, indicated by the symbol on its forehead. 
> 
> Lion turtles possess the *energybending* ability (described in the Monk class). A lion turtle can use this ability to remove a creature's bending of the element the lion turtle is associated with. 
>
> It can also grant a willing creature the ability to bend this element, regardless of whether the target already bends another element, had its bending blocked by energybending, bloodbending, or other means, or is not a bender.
>
> Air lion turtles additionally have a flying speed of 20 ft.
>
> ***Camouflaged Body.*** As it grows larger and older, a lion turtle's shell develops plant life which starts to sustain an entire forest ecosystem. When floating on water, a lion turtle easily passes as a small island.
> ### Actions
> ***Multiattack.*** The lion turtle makes three attacks: one with its bite and two with its claws. It can make one tail attack in place of its two claw attacks.
>
> ***Bite.*** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +13 to hit, reach 15 ft., one target. *Hit:* 26 (3d12 + 7) piercing damage. 
>
> ***Claw.*** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +13 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. *Hit:* 16 (2d8 + 7) slashing damage.
>
> ***Tail.*** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +13 to hit, reach 15 ft., one target. *Hit:* 26 (3d12 + 7) bludgeoning damage. If the target is a creature, it must succeed on a DC 20 Strength saving throw or be pushed up to 10 feet away from the lion turtle and knocked prone.

<h2>The Serpent</h2>

The serpent inhavits the lakes surrounding the Serpent's Pass in the Earth Nation. It generally subsists on aquatic creatures, but will eat anything that comes across its path, including ships and the rare humans who brave the Serpent's Pass. Before attacking, it lets out a bloodcurdling scream.
